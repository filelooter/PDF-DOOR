How can we help you today?

General Questions

Yes, all the core tools on our website are free for everyone. Our service is supported by advertisements and optional donations. This allows us to keep the tools accessible without any cost to you.

Your privacy is our top priority. All tools on PDF Door work client-side, meaning the processing happens directly in your browser. Your files are never uploaded to our servers, so they remain completely private and secure on your device.

No, PDF Door is a fully web-based tool. You don't need to install any software or plugins. All you need is a modern web browser and an internet connection to use all our tools from any device, including desktops, tablets, and smartphones.

Most of our tools are designed for PDF files. However, many of our conversion tools also support other formats like Microsoft Word (.doc, .docx), Excel (.xls, .xlsx), PowerPoint (.ppt, .pptx), and images (.jpg, .png).

Since all processing is done in your browser, the main limitation is your computer's own processing power and memory. Very large or complex files might perform slower on older devices. For our free service, we do not impose strict usage limits for normal use.

Using The Tools

Navigate to the "Merge PDF" tool. Click "Select Files" or drag your PDFs into the upload area. You can reorder the files by dragging them. Once you're ready, click the "Merge PDFs" button, and your new combined file will be ready to download instantly.

The effectiveness of compression depends on the content of the PDF. If your PDF is already highly optimized or contains mainly text, the size reduction might be minimal. Compression works best on PDFs with large, uncompressed images.

You can use our "Organize PDF" or "Split PDF" tools. The "Organize PDF" tool will show you a preview of all pages, which you can drag and drop to reorder or delete. The "Split PDF" tool allows you to extract pages in any order you specify.

It means maximum security and speed. "Client-side" means the work is done by your computer (the "client"), not our servers. Your files don't need to be sent over the internet, processed, and then sent back. This makes the process faster and ensures no one but you ever sees your files.

Go to the "Word to PDF" tool. Select your .doc or .docx file from your computer. The tool will then convert the file within your browser, and a download button for your new PDF will appear once it's complete.

Troubleshooting

Errors can sometimes occur if the PDF file is corrupted, encrypted with a password, or has a very complex structure. Try using our "Repair PDF" tool first. If the problem persists, please contact us with details about the error.

Please try clearing your browser's cache and cookies or using a different web browser. Since all processing is done on your device, a large or complex file might take longer on a less powerful computer. Make sure your internet connection is stable.

Converting documents with complex layouts, fonts, or images can sometimes be challenging. Our tools strive for the best possible accuracy. If you face issues, ensure the source document is well-structured. For PDF to Word conversions, complex tables or graphics might shift slightly.

Most of our tools provide a default name for the processed file (e.g., "merged.pdf"). Some tools may offer an option to set a custom filename before you process the files. Always check for a filename input box in the tool's options.

Ensure you are using a modern web browser like Chrome, Firefox, or Edge. Some older browsers or certain browser extensions (like ad blockers) can sometimes interfere with drag and drop functionality. Try disabling extensions or using a different browser.
